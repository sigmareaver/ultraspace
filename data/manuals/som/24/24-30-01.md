# 24-30-01 — Electrical Power Up

**From:** all systems de-energized (cold and dark).
**To:** BUS E and BUS A powered, equipment loads energized.

WARNING: Bus capacitors retain charge after shutdown. Verify bus voltage
before touching distribution wiring, regardless of switch positions.

CAUTION: Do not close the bus tie onto a de-energized BUS A. See 24-00-00 §3.
The tie will trip on closure transient and the attempt is logged as contact
wear. Use the precharge unit, every time. There is no exception to this
procedure at any battery state.

NOTE 1: Battery contactor closure onto dead BUS E is permitted — the battery
contactor is energization-rated (24-00-00 §2). This permission does NOT
extend to the bus tie.

NOTE 2: The precharge unit refuses to START while the tie is closed
(interlock). If refused, verify tie OPEN and repeat.

## Procedure

Execute per checklist `som-24-30-01`:

```
 1. BUS E voltage ............................ VERIFY < 2 V
 2. BAT 1 contactor .......................... CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS E ................................. 25 V minimum within 2 s
 3. CB E1 (avionics) ......................... CLOSE
       BUS E, loaded ......................... 25 V minimum
 4. Battery current .......................... VERIFY 1.5–2.5 A
 5. BUS A tie ................................ VERIFY OPEN
 6. BUS A precharge .......................... START
       BUS A ................................. 25 V minimum within 5 s
 7. BUS A tie ................................ CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS A ................................. 25.5 V minimum
 8. CB A1 (cabin) ............................ CLOSE
       BUS A, loaded ......................... 25.5–27.5 V
 9. Battery current, all loads ............... VERIFY 6–8 A
```

Expected indications assume BAT 1 at or above 70% state of charge. At lower
charge states all voltages read proportionally lower; below 40% expect
BUS UNDERVOLT annunciation during load application, and refer to the
dispatch limits in the MEL before flight (MEL Ch 24: *pending*).
