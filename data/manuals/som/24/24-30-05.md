# 24-30-05 — Cross-Tie: Feed BUS E from BAT 2 (Abnormal)

**From:** BAT 1 unavailable (failed, depleted, or isolated); BUS E and its
loads de-energized or about to be.
**To:** BUS E fed from BAT 2 through the cross-tie; BUS E loads restored.

Use this procedure when BUS E must be carried by BAT 2. BUS A may
subsequently be brought up from BUS E per 24-30-03 steps 10–13.

CAUTION: Verify the BAT 1 contactor OPEN before closing the cross-tie.
Closing the cross-tie with both battery contactors closed parallels the
batteries (24-00-01 §2).

CAUTION: The precharge unit cannot raise a loaded bus (24-00-00 §3). Shed
every BUS E load before starting precharge; a bus stalled at a few volts
with the unit reporting CHARGING indefinitely means a load is still
connected. Restore loads only after the cross-tie is closed.

NOTE: If the cross-tie has tripped from a prior attempt: RESET it, verify
OPEN, and perform the precharge sequence from the beginning (24-00-00 §3).

## Procedure

Execute per checklist `som-24-30-05`:

```
 1. BAT 1 contactor .......................... VERIFY OPEN
 2. CB E1 (avionics) ......................... OPEN (shed BUS E loads)
 3. BAT 2 contactor .......................... CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS B ................................. 25 V minimum within 2 s
 4. CB B1 (equipment bay) .................... CLOSE
       BUS B, loaded ......................... 25.5 V minimum
 5. Cross-tie (BUS B) ........................ VERIFY OPEN
 6. BUS B precharge .......................... START
       BUS E ................................. 25 V minimum within 5 s
 7. Cross-tie (BUS B) ........................ CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS E ................................. 25.5 V minimum
 8. CB E1 (avionics) ......................... CLOSE
       BUS E, loaded ......................... 25.5–27.5 V
 9. BAT 2 current, both feeders .............. VERIFY 6.5–7.5 A
```

Expected indications assume BAT 2 at or above 70% state of charge. BAT 2
now carries the equipment bay and all BUS E loads; monitor BAT 2 state of
charge and shed load per the dispatch limits in the MEL before flight
(MEL Ch 24: *pending*).
