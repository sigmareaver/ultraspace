# 24-30-03 — Electrical Power Up (UEV Kestrel)

**From:** all systems de-energized (cold and dark).
**To:** BUS E, BUS A, and BUS B powered, equipment loads energized, batteries
split (cross-tie OPEN).

WARNING: Bus capacitors retain charge after shutdown. Verify bus voltage
before touching distribution wiring, regardless of switch positions.

CAUTION: Do not close the BUS A tie onto a de-energized BUS A. See
24-00-00 §3. Use the precharge unit, every time. There is no exception to
this procedure at any battery state.

CAUTION: The cross-tie (BUS E ↔ BUS B) remains OPEN throughout this
procedure. Closing it with both battery contactors closed parallels the
batteries (24-00-01 §2).

NOTE 1: Battery contactor closure onto a dead bus is permitted for BAT 1 and
BAT 2 — battery contactors are energization-rated (24-00-00 §2). This
permission does NOT extend to either tie.

NOTE 2: The precharge units refuse to START while their tie is closed
(interlock). If refused, verify the tie OPEN and repeat.

## Procedure

Execute per checklist `som-24-30-03`:

```
 1. BUS E voltage ............................ VERIFY < 2 V
 2. BAT 1 contactor .......................... CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS E ................................. 25 V minimum within 2 s
 3. CB E1 (avionics) ......................... CLOSE
       BUS E, loaded ......................... 25 V minimum
 4. BAT 1 current ............................ VERIFY 1.5–2.5 A
 5. BUS B voltage ............................ VERIFY < 2 V
 6. BAT 2 contactor .......................... CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS B ................................. 25 V minimum within 2 s
 7. CB B1 (equipment bay) .................... CLOSE
       BUS B, loaded ......................... 25.5 V minimum
 8. BAT 2 current ............................ VERIFY 4.5–5.5 A
 9. Cross-tie (BUS B) ........................ VERIFY OPEN
10. BUS A tie ................................ VERIFY OPEN
11. BUS A precharge .......................... START
       BUS A ................................. 25 V minimum within 5 s
12. BUS A tie ................................ CLOSE (guarded)
       BUS A ................................. 25.5 V minimum
13. CB A1 (cabin) ............................ CLOSE
       BUS A, loaded ......................... 25.5–27.5 V
14. BAT 1 current, all loads ................. VERIFY 6–8 A
```

Expected indications assume both batteries at or above 70% state of charge.
At lower charge states all voltages read proportionally lower; below 40%
expect BUS UNDERVOLT annunciation during load application, and refer to the
dispatch limits in the MEL before flight (MEL Ch 24: *pending*).
