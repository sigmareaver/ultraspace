# 24-00-00 — Electrical Power System

## 1. General

The electrical power system (EPS) stores energy in batteries and distributes
28 V DC power through two buses. BUS E (essential) is fed directly from the
battery contactors. BUS A (equipment) is fed from BUS E through the bus tie
contactor, with a parallel precharge unit.

All distribution passes through panel-mounted circuit breakers. Every load on
the vessel traces to a named breaker; there are no unprotected loads.

## 2. Batteries

Each battery is a 28 V class, 40 Ah unit (P/N 24-101-040). Open-circuit
voltage varies with state of charge, from 28.4 V (full) to 22.0 V (empty).
Expect indicated bus voltage below battery open-circuit voltage when loaded;
the difference is drop across the battery internal resistance and contactor
contacts.

Battery contactors are energization-rated (P/N 24-110-031): their arc-quench
contacts may close onto a fully discharged bus. **No other contactor on the
vessel is energization-rated.**

## 3. Bus tie and precharge

The bus tie contactor (P/N 24-110-030) connects BUS A to BUS E. It is rated
for 30 A continuous and 150 A closure transient.

A de-energized bus presents its full capacitance to the tie at closure. Bus
capacitance charges at a rate limited only by contact resistance; closing the
tie onto a discharged BUS A produces a closure transient exceeding 1000 A —
roughly seven times the contact transient rating. The tie protection will
trip, and repeated attempts wear the contact faces.

The precharge unit (P/N 24-115-050) exists to remove this transient. It
connects a 50 Ω resistor across the tie when commanded to START, charging the
dead bus at not more than approximately 0.6 A. When the voltage difference
across the tie falls below 1.5 V, the unit reports COMPLETE and disconnects
automatically. The tie may then be closed; the closure transient at 1.5 V is
approximately 75 A, within rating.

The precharge unit is interlocked: it will refuse to START while the bus tie
is closed, since a closed tie shorts the precharge path and the sequence
would serve no purpose. A **tripped** tie holds the interlock exactly as a
closed one does not — the tie must be RESET (returning it to OPEN) before
the precharge unit will START and before any further closure is accepted.

**Recovery from a tie trip:** RESET the tie at its address, verify OPEN,
then perform the precharge sequence from the beginning. Each trip is logged
as contact wear; recovery does not erase the log entry.

## 4. Instrumentation

Bus voltages and battery current/state-of-charge are measured by dedicated
transducers and displayed with source and data age. Contactor and breaker
positions are not telemetered on this vessel: verify positions by panel
observation (`read` at the device address reports the panel state).

NOTE: All indications carry transducer tolerance. Do not chase differences
of less than 0.2 V between indicated and expected values.

## 5. Annunciators

| Annunciator | Trigger | Ref |
|---|---|---|
| BUS E UNDERVOLT | BUS E below 24.0 V, after first reaching 25.0 V | 24-30-02 |
| BUS A UNDERVOLT | BUS A below 24.0 V, after first reaching 25.0 V | 24-30-02 |

Undervolt annunciators arm when their bus first passes 25.0 V, and remain
armed. A bus taken down deliberately will therefore annunciate; this is
normal and confirms the monitor is live.
