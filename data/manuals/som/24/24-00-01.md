# 24-00-01 — Electrical Power System, UEV Kestrel Supplement

This section supplements 24-00-00 for the UEV Kestrel. Sections 1–5 of
24-00-00 apply except as amended here.

## 1. General (amended)

The Kestrel distributes 28 V DC power through **three** buses fed by **two**
batteries under a split-bus policy:

- **BUS E** (essential) — fed directly from the BAT 1 contactor.
- **BUS A** (equipment) — fed from BUS E through the BUS A tie contactor,
  with a parallel precharge unit (as on 24-00-00 §3).
- **BUS B** (equipment) — fed directly from the BAT 2 contactor.

BAT 1 carries BUS E and BUS A; BAT 2 carries BUS B. In normal operation the
batteries are **never paralleled**: the cross-tie (below) remains OPEN.

## 2. Cross-tie (BUS E ↔ BUS B)

A cross-tie contactor with its own precharge unit connects BUS E to BUS B for
abnormal operations (single-battery cross-feed). It is addressed at
`eps.bus.b.tie` / `eps.bus.b.precharge` and obeys the same precharge law and
interlock as the BUS A tie (24-00-00 §3).

CAUTION: Closing the cross-tie with both battery contactors closed parallels
BAT 1 and BAT 2. Mismatched state of charge drives circulating current
through both batteries. Cross-feed procedures (QRH Ch 24: *pending*) require
isolating one battery first. In normal operations the cross-tie is verified
OPEN and left OPEN.

## 3. Instrumentation (amended)

BUS B voltage and BAT 2 current/state-of-charge are instrumented identically
to their BUS E / BAT 1 counterparts (24-00-00 §4).

## 4. Annunciators (amended)

| Annunciator | Trigger | Ref |
|---|---|---|
| BUS E UNDERVOLT | BUS E below 24.0 V, after first reaching 25.0 V | 24-30-04 |
| BUS A UNDERVOLT | BUS A below 24.0 V, after first reaching 25.0 V | 24-30-04 |
| BUS B UNDERVOLT | BUS B below 24.0 V, after first reaching 25.0 V | 24-30-04 |

Arming behavior is as described in 24-00-00 §5.
