# WDM 24-00-00 — Electrical Power Wiring Data

This chapter is generated from the vessel blueprint and part catalog; it
cannot disagree with the hardware. Effectivity is per airframe: verify the
title block of the generated sheet matches your hull before troubleshooting.

Generated data sheets for this chapter: [`generated/tb-1.md`](generated/tb-1.md),
[`generated/uev-kestrel.md`](generated/uev-kestrel.md).

## How to use this chapter

- **Panel table** — every switching and protection device, its SCL address,
  and what it sits between. When a procedure says "CB E1", the panel table
  is where that name is bound to hardware.
- **Feeder trees** — the one-line diagram per bus. Power flows left to
  right, away from the bus. Use these to answer "what dies if I open this?"
- **Wire list** — every electrical node and everything landed on it. Use
  during physical fault isolation: a symptom shared by all devices on one
  node points at the node, not the devices.
- **Load list** — nominal currents at 28 V and the protecting device for
  each load. Compare against measured feeder current to detect stray or
  shifted load.
- **Instrumentation** — which transducer feeds which indication, and where
  it physically measures. When an indication disagrees with expectation,
  check here *first* what the reading actually is: no indication is ever
  better than its transducer (SOM 24-00-00 §4).

NOTE: Node capacitance values in the wire list are design data, provided
because they set precharge times and closure transients (SOM 24-00-00 §3).
They are not measurable in-place with panel instrumentation.
